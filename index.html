Overview

Text-to-image diffusion models achieve impressive realism but often fail under compositional prompts with multiple objects, attributes, and spatial relations, leading to cross-token interference where entities entangle, attributes bleed across objects, and spatial cues are violated.

We propose Mask-Attn SDXL, a cross-attention gating mechanism that injects a learnable binary mask directly into the attention logits of SDXL's U-Net. The mask is estimated per token and per layer, sparsifying text-to-image routing so that only semantically relevant token-to-latent channels remain active.

The method requires no positional encodings, auxiliary tokens, or external region masks, and preserves the original inference path with negligible overhead. Fine-tuning on a subset of COCO captions yields measurable improvements in spatial compliance and attribute binding on multi-object prompts while maintaining overall image quality and sample diversity. These results indicate that logit-level masked cross-attention is a simple, data-efficient primitive for stronger disentanglement and boundary fidelity in text-to-image generation, and a complementary building block for future spatially controllable systems.
